# Sources & References

## Ancient Sources

### Greek Sources
- Ctesias of Cnidus (~5th century BCE). *Indica* (fragmentary, preserved in later authors). First Greek description of the manticore based on Persian accounts.
- Aristotle (4th century BCE). *History of Animals*. Brief mention of the manticore (with skepticism).
- Pausanias (2nd century CE). *Description of Greece*. References to exotic beasts.

### Roman Sources
- Pliny the Elder (77-79 CE). *Natural History*, Book 8. Detailed account of the manticore in Roman natural history.
- Aelian (3rd century CE). *On the Nature of Animals*. Collection including manticore descriptions.

## Medieval Sources

### Bestiaries
- Various Medieval Latin Bestiaries (12th-15th centuries). Illuminated manuscripts with manticore illustrations and moral interpretations.
- White, T.H. (1954). *The Book of Beasts: Being a Translation from a Latin Bestiary of the Twelfth Century*. Jonathan Cape. ISBN: 978-0486240848
